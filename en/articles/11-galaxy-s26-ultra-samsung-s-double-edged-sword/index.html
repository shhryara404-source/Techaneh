Galaxy S26 Ultra: Samsung's Double‑Edged Sword

The Galaxy S26 Ultra with Privacy Display, Snapdragon 8 Elite Gen 5, and a 200MP f/1.4 camera is a full‑fledged flagship...

Techaneh Editorial

If Apple is the cautious, quiet empire, Samsung is the roaring army that charges in at full force every year. The Galaxy S26 Ultra was unveiled on February 25, 2026, and hit global markets in March. Samsung has chosen a different path this year: instead of major hardware changes, the focus is on software, AI, and creative features.

Design: At 163.6×78.1×7.9mm and 214g, it's one of the thinnest and most ergonomic heavyweight flagships. The rear camera module has been redesigned with rounder corners. The camera panel uses new glass seamlessly integrated with the Armor Aluminum frame. Display protection comes from Gorilla Glass Armor 2. Colours include black, cobalt violet, sky blue, and white.

Display: A 6.9‑inch Dynamic AMOLED 2X with QHD+ resolution and adaptive 1‑120Hz refresh rate. But Samsung's trump card is the Privacy Display, which limits side viewing angles by controlling light emission at the pixel level.

Chip: The heart of this phone is the Snapdragon 8 Elite Gen 5 for Galaxy, built on 3nm technology. It delivers a 19% CPU boost, 39% NPU boost, and 24% GPU boost.

Camera: Samsung still leads in the camera department:

  • Main: 200MP with f/1.4 aperture
  • Ultrawide: 50MP with f/1.9
  • Telephoto 1: 50MP with 5x optical zoom
  • Telephoto 2: 10MP

The key highlight is the f/1.4 aperture on the main sensor, which captures more light than the previous f/1.7 and takes night photography to a new level.
