MacBook Pro 2026: When Apple Unveils M5 to M7 All at Once

Apple will release three generations of chips in 2026. Meet the M5 Pro/Max, M6 (2nm), and M7...

Techaneh Editorial

2026 is a completely different year for Apple. The company isn't just showcasing a laptop, but a new strategy – one where Apple fundamentally changes its processor roadmap for the first time. In March 2026, Apple unveiled MacBook Pros with M5 Pro and M5 Max chips. But that was just the beginning. In the second half of 2026, the first 2nm M6 laptops arrive, and in the first half of 2027, M7 Pro and M7 Max with a completely new design.

First Generation: M5 Pro & M5 Max (March 2026)

The M5 Pro and M5 Max chips are built with Fusion architecture, combining two silicon dies into one chip. Both feature up to 18 CPU cores (6 Super Cores + 12 performance cores). Unified memory reaches up to 128GB on the M5 Max, and internal storage starts at 1TB. The Liquid Retina XDR display offers a nano‑texture option to reduce glare. Support for Wi‑Fi 7 and Thunderbolt 5. 24‑hour battery life. Pricing starts at $2499 for the 14‑inch and $3499 for the 16‑inch.

Second Generation: M6 (H2 2026)

Apple has made a bold decision: M6 Pro and M6 Max chips are completely cancelled, and only the standard M6 will be released. This is Apple's first 2nm chip for Mac. Memory bandwidth increases from 153 to 200 GB/s, and a redesigned 12‑core GPU is offered. The M6 will initially launch on entry‑level 14‑inch models.

Third Generation: M7 (H1 2027)

The MacBook Pro arrives with a completely redesigned chassis and M7 Pro/Max chips. These models will directly replace the M5 Pro/Max and are set to be the most powerful laptops in Apple's history.

If you need one now → M5 Pro/Max. If you can wait → M6. If you want the absolute best → wait for M7.
